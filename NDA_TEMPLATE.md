## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 **"Confidential Information"** means all information, whether written, oral, electronic, visual, or in any other form, disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, including but not limited to:

   a) **Technical Information**:
      - Source code, algorithms, and software architecture
      - Performance metrics and benchmark results
      - Technical specifications and documentation
      - Data structures and database schemas
      - API designs and implementations
      - Software designs, prototypes, and demos
      
   b) **Intellectual Property**:
      - Patent applications and invention disclosures
      - Trade secrets and proprietary processes
      - Copyrighted materials and documentation
      - Trademarks and branding materials
      
   c) **Business Information**:
      - Business plans and strategies
      - Financial information and projections
      - Customer lists and contacts
      - Pricing information and terms
      - Marketing plans and materials
      - Partnerships and relationships
      
   d) **Product Information**:
      - Product roadmaps and features
      - Development schedules and milestones
      - Beta testing results and feedback
      - User research and analytics

1.2 Confidential Information includes information that is:
   - Marked as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar designation
   - Identified as confidential at the time of disclosure
   - Reasonably understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure

1.3 Confidential Information specifically includes all information related to the **Queztl-Core Software GPU Technology**, including:
   - Software GPU architecture with thread block simulation
   - Vectorized kernel execution engine
   - Quantum prediction engine for optimization
   - Quad-linked list data structure
   - WebGPU and OpenGL compatibility layers
   - Web-native RESTful GPU API
   - AI swarm coordination system
   - All associated algorithms, code, and documentation

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

2.1 **Non-Disclosure**: The Receiving Party agrees to:
   a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence
   b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
   c) Not use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose
   d) Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but in no case less than reasonable care

2.2 **Limited Access**: The Receiving Party shall:
   a) Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, or agents who have a legitimate need to know
   b) Ensure all such persons are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as this Agreement
   c) Be responsible for any breaches by such persons

2.3 **Security Measures**: The Receiving Party shall:
   a) Store Confidential Information securely using industry-standard encryption
   b) Not copy, reproduce, or reverse engineer Confidential Information without written permission
   c) Not decompile, disassemble, or attempt to derive source code from any software
   d) Use secure communication channels when discussing Confidential Information
   e) Implement two-factor authentication on all accounts accessing Confidential Information

2.4 **No Rights Granted**: This Agreement does not grant the Receiving Party any rights to:
   a) Use, copy, modify, or distribute Confidential Information beyond the Purpose
   b) Create derivative works based on Confidential Information
   c) File patent applications based on Confidential Information
   d) Commercialize or sell products incorporating Confidential Information

## 3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that:

3.1 Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

3.2 Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

3.3 Is rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation;

3.4 Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

3.5 Is approved for release by written authorization of the Disclosing Party.

---

## 4. REQUIRED DISCLOSURES

4.1 If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall:
   a) Immediately notify the Disclosing Party in writing
   b) Cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order
   c) Disclose only the minimum information required
   d) Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment

---

## 5. RETURN OF MATERIALS

5.1 Upon request by the Disclosing Party, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall:
   a) Immediately return or destroy all Confidential Information in any form
   b) Delete all electronic copies from computers, servers, and backup systems
   c) Provide written certification of compliance within 10 business days
   d) Continue to be bound by confidentiality obligations despite return/destruction

## 6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

6.1 **Ownership**: All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party. No license or other rights are granted except as explicitly stated in this Agreement.

6.2 **Patent Rights**: The Receiving Party acknowledges that:
   a) The Disclosing Party has filed or intends to file patent applications
   b) The Receiving Party shall not file any patent applications based on Confidential Information
   c) The Receiving Party shall not contest the validity of any patents or patent applications
   d) The Receiving Party shall not assist others in contesting such patents

6.3 **Trade Secrets**: The Receiving Party acknowledges that Confidential Information includes trade secrets protected under the Uniform Trade Secrets Act and federal law.

6.4 **Inventions**: Any inventions, improvements, or discoveries made by the Receiving Party that are based on or derived from Confidential Information shall be the sole property of the Disclosing Party.

## 8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 **Term**: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for a period of **five (5) years**, unless terminated earlier by mutual written agreement.

8.2 **Survival**: The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination for:
   a) **Trade secrets**: As long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law
   b) **Patent-pending information**: Until patents issue or applications are abandoned
   c) **Other Confidential Information**: For a period of five (5) years after termination

8.3 **Termination**: Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days' written notice, provided that confidentiality obligations continue as specified above.

9.1 **Irreparable Harm**: The Receiving Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement will cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party that cannot be adequately compensated by monetary damages.

9.2 **Injunctive Relief**: The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek:
   a) Immediate injunctive relief to prevent or restrain breach
   b) Specific performance of this Agreement
   c) Such other equitable relief as the court deems appropriate

9.3 **Damages**: In addition to injunctive relief, the Disclosing Party may seek:
   a) Actual damages resulting from breach
   b) Consequential damages, including lost profits
   c) Statutory damages under applicable trade secret laws
   d) Attorneys' fees and costs of enforcement

9.4 **Criminal Penalties**: The Receiving Party acknowledges that unauthorized disclosure may constitute:
   a) Violation of the Economic Espionage Act (18 U.S.C. ¬ß 1831-1839)
   b) Computer Fraud and Abuse Act violations (18 U.S.C. ¬ß 1030)
   c) State trade secret law violations
